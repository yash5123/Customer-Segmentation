Customer Segmentation Ledger

Classify customer attributes to identify behavioral segments and targeted marketing strategies.

This tool sorts customers into six behavioral groups discovered in actual shopping data, so a business can understand who its customers are and market to each group differently.

How It Works

  1. 01

    Data Collection

    Real shopping data from customers (their age, income, and spending habits) was collected and prepared.

  2. 02

    Clustering Analysis

    A machine learning method grouped those customers into six segments based on shared patterns, without being told in advance what the groups should be.

  3. 03

    Persona Profiling

    Each segment was studied and given a plain-language description and marketing strategy based on who its customers actually are.

  4. 04

    Interactive Mapping

    Enter a customer's details below to see which segment they fall into and how they compare to the average.

The Six Customer Groups

Spender Apprentices

Younger shoppers with entry level incomes who spend heavily.

Young Moderates

Younger adults earning average salaries with balanced spending.

High-Value Trendsetters

Younger, high earning shoppers who spend heavily.

Affluent Conservatives

High earning middle aged adults who spend very conservatively.

Budget Conscious

Middle aged shoppers with lower incomes who spend conservatively.

Traditionalists

Older shoppers with moderate incomes who spend moderately.

Model Mechanics

The segmentation is driven by a K-Means clustering algorithm, a machine learning model that finds natural groupings in multi-dimensional customer data. The classification process runs as follows:

  • Feature Scaling: Customer age, annual income, and spending scores are measured on different scales. To prevent larger numbers (like income) from dominating the calculation, all values are normalized to a standard scale.
  • Distance Minimization: The algorithm establishes six center points (centroids) in this standardized space. It then assigns each customer to the closest center point based on geometric distance.
  • Iterative Refinement: The center points are adjusted repeatedly until they sit perfectly in the middle of their respective customer clusters, minimizing the total distance from customers to their assigned segment center.
  • Real-Time Assignment: When you submit a new profile, the system scales the input variables using the same criteria and calculates which of the six cluster centers is nearest, instantly classifying the customer.
